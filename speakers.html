Guide for Parents

In many ways, your low birth weight baby (LBW), is just like any other baby. She needs to stay warm and dry. She needs to be kept clean, and have enough fluids and nourishment. Above all, she needs you, and the comfort of your love and care.
Breastfeeding is the best way of nourishing your baby. Try not to feed him anything other than breast milk for the first six months after birth.
To help your baby gain weight your child's paediatrician will recommend you feed your baby on schedule rather than on demand. So keep a record of when she has had her milk, and feed her at regular intervals to ensure that she gets her feeds at the right time.
Parents of low birth weight babies are often told to feed their baby at least every three hours, sometimes every two, even if that means waking your baby from a deep sleep or her nap.
Skin-to-skin contact benefits both you and your LBW baby. Holding your baby directly against your skin, which is also called kangaroo care, is known to: help her gain weight maintain her body warmth regulate her heart and breathing rates help her spend more time in deep sleep give a better chance of successful breastfeeding help her spend more time being quiet and alert and less time crying
